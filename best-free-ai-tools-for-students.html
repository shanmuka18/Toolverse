1. Research & Study Tools

These tools help you understand concepts faster, find reliable sources, and prepare for exams without drowning in PDFs.

📓 NotebookLM Free

Google's AI tool that turns your own notes, PDFs, and textbooks into a personal tutor. Upload your lecture slides and ask it anything — it only answers from your uploaded material, so no hallucinations. The Audio Overview feature turns any document into a podcast-style summary. Easily the most powerful free study tool available right now.

🔬 Consensus Free

Search millions of real academic papers and get AI-summarized answers with citations. Perfect for essay introductions and literature reviews. Unlike Google, every result is a peer-reviewed paper — not a random blog post. Free tier gives you 20 searches per day.

🃏 Quizlet AI Freemium

Auto-generates flashcards, practice tests, and quizzes from any topic or your uploaded notes. The AI explanation feature breaks down why an answer is correct. Trusted by over 500 million students worldwide. Free plan covers most study needs.

2. Writing & Essay Tools

From fixing grammar to structuring arguments, these tools help you write better essays and reports in less time.

✏️ Grammarly Freemium

The industry standard for student writing. Catches grammar and spelling mistakes, suggests clearer phrasing, and improves your overall writing tone. Works as a browser extension so it helps you everywhere — Google Docs, emails, and even LinkedIn. The free plan is genuinely useful and more than enough for most students.

WritingGrammarEssays
Try Free →
🔍 Perplexity AI Free

Think of it as a smarter Google. Ask any research question and get a summarized answer with real cited sources you can verify. Great for quickly understanding a topic before writing about it. Far more reliable than asking ChatGPT directly, since every claim is linked to a source.

ResearchWritingSources
Try Free →

💡 Pro tip: Use Perplexity to find sources and understand the topic, then use Grammarly to polish your final essay. Together they cover the full writing workflow for free.

3. Coding Tools

Whether you're in your first programming class or building projects for your portfolio, these tools will make you code faster and understand errors instantly.

👨‍💻 GitHub Copilot Free for Students

The world's most used AI coding assistant — and it's completely free for students with a .edu email or GitHub Student Developer Pack. Autocompletes code, explains errors, and suggests entire functions as you type. Works inside VS Code. If you write any code at all, this is a must-have.

Build full web apps just by describing what you want in plain English. No setup, no terminal, no config. Great for students who want to build portfolio projects without getting stuck on environment issues. The free tier gives you enough credits to build and demo a working project.
